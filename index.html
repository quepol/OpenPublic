What I do.

Successful public service delivery depends on leaders fluent in the language of delivery, teams with the skills to manage outcomes, and governance that clears the path—whether work is done in-house or with partners. Too often, leaders are accountable for results without knowing what good looks like, what questions to ask, or when they're being sold expensive theater.

Public institutions deliver when policy, design, and technology work together—not sequential handoffs, but integrated teams. This requires structures that enable multidisciplinary co-creation, governance that supports test-and-learn approaches, and relentless focus on end users. Without these conditions, even the best policy intent produces little.

My work sits in that gap between mandate and execution. I help leaders build delivery capacity in practice: clarifying decision rights, standing up empowered cross-functional teams, and designing governance that allows policy, funding, and delivery to move together rather than in silos. I'm most often brought in when programs are politically sensitive, vendor-heavy, or stuck—when success depends less on hiring more consultants and more on building the muscle to deliver.

About me.

I've spent my career working at the intersection of government, technology, and leadership—co-founding 18F, serving as Ontario's first Chief Digital and Data Officer, leading U.S. Digital Response, and partnering with governments at all levels.

Across these roles, I've seen the same pattern: initiatives fail not because teams lack talent or intent, but because institutions aren't designed to support delivery. My work is grounded in a simple belief—governments and public-interest institutions earn trust through reliability.

That requires leaders fluent in the language of delivery, teams with the skills to manage outcomes, and governance that clears the path—whether you're building in-house or working with partners.

Training & Workshops

Practical, leadership-focused workshops designed to build shared understanding of what modern digital delivery looks like in government and what it takes to enable it. Grounded in real public-sector experience, these sessions create a common language across disciplines and equip participants with concrete ways to reduce risk, remove friction, and improve how services are designed and delivered.

All workshops are delivered personally by Hillary Hartley.

Workshops

Foundations of Modern Digital Leadership

An interactive workshop that helps public-sector leaders understand their role in modern digital delivery—and how leadership behaviours, decisions, and incentives directly shape outcomes.

Who it's for: Executives, senior leaders, directors and managers accountable for programs or services, policy leaders, HR and central agency leaders, aspiring leaders moving into delivery roles

What you'll work on:

  • Understanding why "every leader is digital" and the shift from oversight to enablement
  • Learning to ask better questions about users, feedback, risk, and progress
  • Creating conditions for success through empowered teams and aligned governance
  • Building psychological safety and learning from failure as leadership responsibilities

Duration: 1.5–2 hours
Format: Interactive presentation with Q&A and participation prompts. Can be delivered remote or in-person.

Foundations of a Modern Digital Workforce

A practical workshop that helps leaders and teams understand what it takes to build, support, and sustain a modern digital workforce in government.

Who it's for: Executives and senior managers, policy and program owners, HR and talent teams, digital and IT leaders, central agency staff

What you'll work on:

  • Understanding what "modern digital work" really means beyond technology
  • Building multidisciplinary teams with user research, service design, product management, and agile delivery capabilities
  • Identifying structural barriers like fragmentation, rigid processes, and misaligned incentives
  • Using policies, governance, and culture as levers to create better conditions for teams

Duration: 1.5–2 hours
Format: Interactive presentation with Q&A and participation prompts. Can be delivered remote or in-person.

Enabling Modern Digital Delivery

A facilitated discussion for teams ready to move from understanding to action—identifying real barriers, mapping them to the levers you control, and designing small, testable changes that improve conditions for delivery.

Who it's for: Leaders, mixed teams, or central agencies who want to turn insight into concrete next steps

What you'll work on:

  • Identifying where approvals, governance, hiring, or funding create delivery friction
  • Understanding what different roles and teams control and how decisions create constraints or opportunities
  • Mapping real challenges to practical system, process, or behaviour changes
  • Designing low-risk pilots and experiments teams can run within their current authority

Duration: 1.5–2 hours
Format: Interactive presentation with facilitated discussion. Can be delivered remote or in-person.
